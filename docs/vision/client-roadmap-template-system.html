Why This Exists

Every RPI client deserves the same quality of preparation. The current pattern — hand-building one-off HTML inventories per client — doesn’t scale past 20 cases and loses the consistency that makes RPI’s service promise credible.

1The Problem

Kim Ruth's Client Roadmap (shipped 2026-04-09) was a one-off. 9 tabs, hand-crafted HTML, case-specific field names, ~800 lines of markup. Beautiful for one client. Impossible to replicate consistently for the next 100.

The same problem exists in Discovery meetings, Analysis + Recommendation meetings, and Implementation onboarding. Every time we build a client-facing artifact from scratch, we:

  • Lose consistency (different Advisors produce different documents)
  • Lose brand voice (every pass drifts from The RPI Way™)
  • Duplicate effort (same structure rebuilt 100 times)
  • Block reuse across modalities (screen + print + web are separate workstreams)

2The Approach

Create + Register WHILE Building. Every reusable pattern becomes a named template with an ID, an owner, a version, and a registry entry. When we find a new pattern — a new VIEW, a new meeting structure, a new story — we promote it to the registry and it becomes available to every future client case.

The Kim Ruth work becomes the first populated instance of the templates, not the templates themselves. The templates live in docs/templates/ and are referenced (not copied) by individual client cases.

The 3 Stages

Every client is in one of three stages. The Analysis template adapts to the client's current stage — surfacing relevant VIEWs and suppressing irrelevant ones.
PROSPER
Accumulation Phase · typical age 30s–50s

Client is building wealth. Still working. Growth is the priority. Estate planning is present but not urgent.

PRESERVE
Transition Phase · typical age 55–75

Client is at or near retirement. Protecting what they've built while still growing select assets. Income planning becomes critical. LTC readiness matters.

PROTECT
Distribution + Legacy Phase · typical age 70+

Client is drawing income. Focused on transfer strategy, estate completeness, and charitable intent. Growth is secondary to income durability and legacy optimization.

Kim Ruth is in PRESERVE. 71-year-old farmer, single, no children, sister as sole beneficiary. His A+R meeting next week uses tpl.preserve-stage-analysis with all 8 VIEWs surfaced.
