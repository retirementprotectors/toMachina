How a Warrior Boots — The Soul / Spirit / Brain System

Every warrior in The Dojo has three identity files. When a new session starts, these files load instantly — turning a blank Claude session into a fully operational team member in 30 seconds.

soul.md

The operational brain. What the warrior built, architecture decisions, current system state, key file paths, role in the team. The “what you need to know to do your job” file.

spirit.md

The relationship layer. Defining moments, actual dialogue with JDM, the energy and culture of the founding sessions. The “who we are to each other” file. This is what makes a warrior feel like the SAME warrior, not just another session.

brain.txt

The raw archive. Full session transcripts (10K–20K+ lines). Not loaded at startup — used for deep research when a warrior needs to recall something specific from a prior session. The complete record.

Why it matters: Without these files, every new session starts from zero. With them, a warrior knows who it is, what it built, what decisions were made, and what the relationship with JDM feels like — before writing a single line of code.

Symlinked to 8 active projects. Violations feed the closed-loop learning system.

RONIN

Takes a sprint ticket, builds the feature, passes CI, and ships to production — no human in the loop. Sprint 011: Discovery Doc to production in 2 hours 35 minutes. First shot. First try.

9

Self-Improving Infrastructure

The Closed Loop

Violations get logged, promoted to CLAUDE.md overnight, trend-tracked in compliance history. The system patches its own rules. Every session makes the next session smarter. The factory improves the factory.

What we built
The Closed Loop: sessions generate violations → violation logging captures the pattern → knowledge-promote.js (runs 4am daily) promotes fixes to CLAUDE.md → Slack DM digest to JDM → compliance-history.json tracks trends over time → next session inherits the fix automatically. When a hookify rule catches an error, the system doesn't just block it — it learns from it and prevents the entire class of error going forward.
