01Casework Doctrine

The 4 product-selection rules

Locked during Ruth A+R prep. Every annuity recommendation runs through these before anything else.

Rule 1
Bonus products only to overcome surrender

Minimum bonus needed, no more. A 21% bonus on a 0%-surrender source = wasted performance over 10yr because carriers pay back bonuses through lower caps/participation rates.

Rule 2
Avoid rider fees on accumulation products

Income products have rider fees (deliberate — buying income). Accumulation should be fee-clean unless bonus requires EBR to cover surrender.

Rule 3
IRAs consolidate into IPP10 with GMIB for QCD strategy

Traditional IRAs roll into NAC Income Pay Pro 10. GMIB base powers QCD (Qualified Charitable Distribution) — RMDs sent direct to church/charity, no income tax, within $108K QCD annual limit.

Rule 4 / 7
NQ consolidates into CP10 base (no EBR, no rider fee)

Non-qualified money → NAC CP10 base. 12% bonus on premiums received in first 3 years. Flex-premium structure lets CDs be added as they mature without starting new contracts. No EBR when source is liquid.

What NOT to touch (Ruth case)

  • SPDA in-surrender window — let it run out
  • SPIA on another life — can't move
  • Roth — never touch

Flex-premium SPDA capture

CP10's flex-premium design means CDs maturing at staggered dates can land into a single NQ contract as they come due — no new contract per CD, no reset of surrender schedule clock for future adds. This is how the SPDA (when it exits surrender) gets absorbed into the same CP10 that already holds the liquid NQ pile.

Split-LPA framing

Liquid Premium Account disposition is split deliberately: the portion matching current-year cashflow needs stays liquid (no annuity wrap); the remainder rolls to CP10 base as consolidation capacity. Framing prevents over-annuitization and preserves emergency-access posture.

EBR kill-shot math

On a 0%-surrender source, EBR cost structure (typically ~1.15%/yr on benefit base) compounds against cap/participation headwinds from the bonus itself. Over 10yr the bonus dollars are recaptured by the carrier and the rider fee is pure drag. EBR only earns its keep when it's offsetting a real surrender charge on the source.

Contract anniversary × RMD alignment

IPP10 GMIB rollup and RMD timing must be aligned at contract setup: anniversary dates that fall after the RMD deadline force awkward bridge distributions. Setup-time decision, not a post-issue fix. Advisor checklist: confirm anniversary month ≤ RMD deadline month before binding.

270–400 bps validation band

Illustration runs that project net of fees outside the 270–400 bps corridor against the underlying index are treated as stress-test outliers, not central cases. Anything above 400 bps requires re-running the assumption set; below 270 bps gets flagged as conservative but acceptable. This band was tested against live NAC and Nassau illustrations during #495 #496.

Why this matters Carrier marketing pushes bonus + EBR + rider stacks because they increase carrier margins, not client outcomes. The doctrine optimizes for long-run client performance, not Day-1 headline numbers.

02Case Format Template

Canonical 5-tab structure

  1. AGENDA — meeting agenda, attendees, objectives
  2. AI³ — asset + insurance + income + beneficiary/RMD inventory (the what)
  3. Analysis (TAIL) — analytical deep-dives across four dimensions (the thinking)
  4. Recommendations — product recommendations with doctrine applied (the output)
  5. Next Steps — action items + timeline

AI³ sub-structure (inventory)

  • Assets — Investable (Type / Tax / Target) · Real (Ag / AgRes / Commercial) · Total
  • Insurance — Medicare · Life · Ancillary
  • Income — Type · Tax
  • Beneficiaries — Beni Center
  • RMD Center

TAIL sub-structure (analysis)

  • Tax — Income · Assets
  • Assets — S/L/G (short/long/gain) · Type · Tax
  • Income — Type · Tax
  • Legacy — C3 (charity/children/care) · Legal (POA/Trust/Will)

Why the mnemonics are deliberate

AI³ = three A/I domains (Assets, Insurance, Income), superscript because no clean 4-letter word exists from A-I-T-E. TAIL = Tax, Assets, Income, Legacy — a real English word, chosen over AITL for that reason. Every case has all four TAIL dimensions by definition; empty quadrants signal scaffolding for future content, not missing information.

Why Tax-view is load-bearing

Tax is the first TAIL column because every downstream decision (asset allocation, income sequencing, legacy vehicle) is tax-constrained. Building Tax-view last means re-doing the rest. The advisor's thinking should surface tax first, then map Assets/Income/Legacy against the tax lens — not the other way around.

Why C3 + Legal gap-columns = the punch list

Legacy tab's C3 (charity/children/care) and Legal (POA/Trust/Will) columns are explicitly gap-columns: they surface what the client has not done. Empty cells there aren't neutral — they're action items. A case with four blank Legal cells is a client who needs estate work before any annuity strategy closes. The format forces the punch list into view.

Format lock Scaffold the 5 tabs + AI³ + TAIL sub-structure first, then migrate content into slots. Placeholder stubs in empty slots are preferred over forcing content into wrong slots. 03Decision Governance

Class-of-bug scope expansion is JDM-gate territory

Individual ticket resolution is warrior lane. When a single bug surfaces a systemic pattern, pause before filing a remediation epic — surface to JDM first.

The three-step pattern

  1. Detection → warrior lane (find the pattern, count it, understand it)
  2. Authorization → JDM-gate (is this breakage or intentional? should we fix it?)
  3. Execution → warrior lane (once authorized, remediate)

Skipping step 2 and jumping from detection to execution is the failure mode.

Why

2026-04-15 — MEGAZORD's ACF audit surfaced "409 broken folder refs" and filed a remediation ZRD treating it as breakage. Trash state may have been intentional (offboarding, archive lifecycle, deliberate cleanup). Scope-expanded remediation would have overwritten intentional state — worse than doing nothing. Warriors moving fast on well-intentioned remediation is only valuable when the fault-finding is correct.

Scales beyond ACF

Applies to dup detection, schema backfills, hygiene epics, any warrior-initiated remediation sprint. Warrior-initiated detection is fine; warrior-initiated scope-expansion into an epic is not. Hold is cheap. Remediation that overwrites intentional state is expensive.

Vendor-cost decisions are JDM-gate territory

Any action that creates or materially changes a recurring vendor charge (API credits, SaaS seats, carrier fees, data-feed subscriptions) requires explicit JDM authorization before execution — regardless of the dollar amount per-call. Warrior estimates of "this is small" are not a substitute for the gate.

The warrior's job: surface cost shape (one-time vs recurring, per-call vs flat), annualized estimate, and cheaper alternatives considered. JDM's job: approve or reject. This prevents death-by-thousand-cuts on the monthly bill.

Posture Warrior = detection + options + execution. JDM = authorization on class-of-bug remediation + recurring vendor cost. The gate is the difference between individual-ticket velocity (good) and unauthorized epic-creation (bad).
