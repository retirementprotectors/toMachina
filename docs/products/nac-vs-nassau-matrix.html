The bonus-accumulation head-to-head, honest read

NAC CP10 + EBR wins on max bonus (21% vs 10%) for clients 0–80. That's the only reason to pick it: the source money has real surrender charges and the 21% upfront offsets them. Every other metric points to Nassau Bonus Annuity — better fixed rate (3.70% vs 3.00%), better S&P cap (8% vs 6%), better S&P participation (40/51% vs 20/25%), Nasdaq-100 exposure NAC doesn't offer, and an optional income rider NAC's CP10 doesn't carry at all. At ages 81–85 Nassau is the only bonus play — EBR isn't available, and Nassau still pays 7%.

Sales-desk rule: if source money has surrender charges and client is 0–80 → NAC CP10 + EBR for max offset. If source is liquid OR client is 81+ OR client wants an income rider attached → Nassau Bonus Annuity.

The no-bonus accumulation head-to-head, honest read

Nassau Growth Annuity wins on headline rates: 5.00% fixed, 11% S&P cap, 62% S&P annual part, 7-year option. If the pitch is “I'll give you my best accumulation numbers,” Nassau Growth Annuity is the honest answer.

BUT — NAC VersaChoice 10 (High band) still wins the volatility-control-index participation race: MS DGI 2-yr at 260% enhanced (0.95% fee) vs Nassau's Sunrise 2-yr at 205% enhanced (1.00% fee). That 55% participation delta matters when the index actually credits — and MS DGI has a longer track record.

NAC Control X is the simplest-pitch winner — 3.90% fixed is the highest declared fixed in the NAC book, and the Barclays 12VC 2-yr margin strategy is a different kind of product (4% margin + 165% participation) that appeals to clients who find Sunrise confusing.

Sales-desk rule: client wants strongest declared rates / Nasdaq-100 exposure / a 7-yr option / or an income-rider attachment → Nassau Growth Annuity. Client wants max volatility-control-index participation / diversified strategy menu / MS DGI specifically → NAC VersaChoice 10 High. Client wants cleanest conversation and highest fixed → NAC Control X.

The income-rider head-to-head, honest read

NAC owns this lane when the client's focus is income. BS10's 120/150/180% benefit base stack is the single strongest guaranteed-income-base growth feature in our carrier lineup, and IPP10 is the cleanest 5–10yr alternative. Nassau's Amplified Income Plus Rider is a feature on top of an accumulation chassis, not a dedicated income product.

Sales-desk rule: client-conversation is “what guaranteed income will I get and when?” → NAC. Client conversation is “I want this accumulation product but also income as a safety net” → Nassau. Different shape, different winner.

Flyer gap: Nassau Amplified Income Plus Rider specs (rollup %, payout %, benefit base structure) need full parse. Until then this comparison is directionally correct but not numerically tight.
