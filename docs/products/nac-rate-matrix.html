Volatility-Control Index Participation Tells the Real Story

On the flagship Morgan Stanley Dynamic Global 2-year strategy: Charter Plus 10 = 130% participation (with bonus). VersaChoice 10 High band = 200% base / 260% enhanced. That's a 70–130% participation delta. If MS DGI runs +8% gross, CP10 credits 10.4% and VersaChoice Enh credits 20.8%. Over 10 years of crediting, the bonus gives up more than it pays.

The honest math: bonus wins when source money comes from a surrender-charged contract (the bonus offsets real surrender dollars). No-bonus wins when source is liquid.

Allocation Rule of Thumb

Recommended allocation mix for an accumulation-focused client: 70% base strategies (no fee) + 30% enhanced strategies (0.95% fee). Over the 10-year surrender, enhanced fees will cost ~$2,850 per $100K allocated, versus ~$6,000+ of additional indexed credits in a mid-case scenario.

6. Comparative Cross-Cuts — The Four Conversations That Matter Most

A. Bonus vs No-Bonus (Same Surrender): Charter Plus 10 vs VersaChoice 10 High Band

Both are 10-year surrender accumulation FIAs. The comparison:

  • CP10: 12% base bonus + optional 9% EBR = up to 21% upfront | Fixed 3.00% | S&P cap 6.00% | MS DGI 2-yr part 130%
  • VersaChoice 10 High: No bonus | Fixed 3.25% | S&P cap 9.20% | MS DGI 2-yr part 200% (base) / 260% (Enh+0.95% fee)
  • Write CP10 when: incoming money carries surrender charges or is otherwise cost-bound; the bonus pays real offset dollars
  • Write VersaChoice when: incoming money is liquid (matured CDs, paid-up annuities, cash); the bonus is a gift we pay for over 10 years in lower crediting
B. Income vs Accumulation (Same Surrender): BenefitSolutions 10 vs VersaChoice 10

Both 10-year surrender. Both NAC. Completely different pitches:

  • BS10: Rider does the work. Benefit base grows on a stacking schedule (120→150→180%), payout is off the benefit base. Crediting on the accumulation value is weaker (5.00% S&P cap vs 9.20%). Client trades accumulation growth for guaranteed income base growth.
  • VersaChoice 10: No rider. All growth shows up in the accumulation value directly. 9.20% S&P cap + 200% MS DGI 2-yr base gives ~2× the accumulation capacity of BS10.
  • Rule: if the client's conversation is “what income will I get?”, BS10 wins. If it's “how much will this be worth?”, VersaChoice wins. Don't mix the pitches.
C. Simple vs Complex Accumulation: Control X vs VersaChoice 10 (High Band)
  • Control X: 3.90% fixed (highest in lineup) | 9.00% S&P cap | 14.00% S&P 2-yr cap | Barclays 12VC 2-yr index margin 4% + 165% participation | FEWER strategies, cleaner client conversation
  • VersaChoice 10: 3.25% fixed | 9.20% S&P cap | 20+ strategies including Fidelity, MS DGI, Goldman, S&P MARC | Enhanced participation rates available for 0.95% fee
  • Pick Control X when: client is older, overwhelmed by option menus, or wants “one simple product” pitch — and you can get them on the Barclays 12VC margin strategy as the growth driver (17.14% hypothetical last-10yr)
  • Pick VersaChoice when: client is engaged, wants diversified crediting across multiple indices, or will use enhanced strategies meaningfully
D. Shorter vs Longer Surrender Accumulation: Performance Choice 8 vs VersaChoice 10 / Control X
  • PC8 trades 2 years of lock for: slightly lower fixed (3.20% vs 3.25%) | slightly lower S&P cap (8.00% vs 9.20%) | essentially the same volatility-control participation (135% / 195% MS DGI base)
  • Pick PC8 when: client is 73–78 and the extra 2 years of lock meaningfully crosses their life expectancy threshold, or when the 10-year lock is itself the objection
  • Pick VersaChoice 10 High when: the $75K+ band applies — you get materially better base rates for the same surrender you'd have anyway (clients don't typically surrender on year 9 if they haven't on year 7)
