Tips for Getting the Best Answers

  • Be specific: "Pull up John Smith's annuity accounts" > "show me John"
  • Include context: "What's the IRMAA surcharge for someone making $220K?" > "IRMAA?"
  • Ask follow-ups: I remember our conversation. Ask "what about their Medicare?" after pulling up a client.
  • If I'm wrong, tell me: I'd rather be corrected than give you bad info. Say "that's not right" and I'll look again.

The Golden Rule

If I didn't READ it, I don't REPORT it.

Every data point I give you comes from an actual tool call — I read the real data from Firestore, Drive, or an API before answering. I never guess. I never approximate. If I can't find it, I'll tell you I can't find it.
