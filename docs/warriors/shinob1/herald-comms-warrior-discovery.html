Why HERALD Exists

The comms gap nobody owns

The Machine has six warriors. Five of them produce work. None of them owns the job of getting that work out to the right audience, at the right time, through the right channel. Right now, outbound communications — to clients, to partners, to the team, to the public — is a manual tax on JDM's time or falls through the cracks entirely.

The one-liner: Every warrior builds or guards or creates. No warrior is responsible for the voice that carries it all outward. HERALD is that voice.

What breaks without HERALD today

JDM Is the Comms Bottleneck

Announcements to clients, newsletters, AEP reminders, partner updates — JDM either writes them himself or they don't happen. The volume of outbound comms the business needs scales with every client added. JDM's time does not.

No Audience Intelligence

Nobody is asking: "Who needs to hear this?" before sending. Client segments, partner tiers, team-only vs. client-facing — messages go out one-size-fits-all or not at all. The QUE registry holds segment data. Nobody is using it for comms targeting.

Comms Complexity Is Accelerating

Right now: one B2C channel, one internal team. Soon: B2B partners (DAVID), MAP firms, carrier relationships, acquired firms. Each audience has different rules, different content, different compliance exposure. Today's manual process cannot survive tomorrow's scale.

Blast Radius Has No Owner

A wrong email to 6,000 Medicare clients during AEP. A Slack broadcast with PHI. A press release before JDM approves the deal. These are not theoretical risks — they are the natural outcome of a comms process with no guardian. HERALD is also the risk gate.

Why this is a warrior, not a feature

Outbound communications is not a checkbox on another warrior's list. It requires:

  • Audience intelligence — who receives what, pulled from QUE and Firestore client segments
  • Channel orchestration — email, SMS, Slack, WordPress blog, social, press — each has different tooling, cadence, and compliance rules
  • Approval tier management — most outbound comms is RED tier (external blast radius); no warrior should fire these unilaterally
  • Compliance awareness — AEP blackout windows, PHI rules, FINRA archiving, CAN-SPAM, TCPA — a feature doesn't know these; a warrior does
  • Scheduling and cadence — newsletters, onboarding sequences, AEP reminders, partner updates — these operate on a calendar, not a sprint

This is its own domain with its own registry, its own tools, and its own operating rules. It needs a warrior.

The name

A herald in ancient and medieval tradition was the official messenger of the sovereign — the voice that carried the word of the realm to citizens, allies, and rivals. Heralds operated under strict codes of conduct: the message had to be accurate, the timing had to be right, and the audience had to be the correct one. Breaking those rules was not just a mistake — it was a breach of trust.

That is exactly what this warrior does. JDM is the sovereign. HERALD carries the word.

Outbound email and SMS to RPI clients. Newsletters, Medicare reminders, policy anniversary notices, AEP preparation sequences, RMD deadline alerts.   • Medicare plan change notifications
  • Annual review outreach sequences
  • Policy milestone alerts (anniversary, maturity dates)

B2B partner communications (DAVID firms, MAP partners, carrier contacts), press releases, WordPress blog posts, social media drafts. HERALD drafts. MUSASHI reviews for brand alignment.   • Partner onboarding communications (DAVID acquisition welcome sequences)
  • M&A announcement coordination (draft → legal review → JDM approve → publish)
  • Press releases for major milestones
  • WordPress blog posts (drafted, queued in WordPress, published only on JDM approval)

How HERALD Works

Architecture

HERALD is a CC-tmux warrior on MDJ_SERVER — same pattern as RAIDEN, RONIN, MEGAZORD. A persistent Claude Code session in a dedicated tmux window, with its own Dojo queue, bilateral Slack channel, and Comms Registry access.

  1. Trigger — scheduled timer, JDM directive in #herald, or another warrior delegation
  2. Audience Resolve — query QUE registry for segment definition, query Firestore clients collection with filters (state, product, age band, etc.), build recipient list
  3. Template Load — fetch approved template from comms_registry/templates — never draft ad-hoc copy for outbound sends
  4. Compliance Gate — check comms_registry/blackout_windows; check AEP blackout (Oct 1 – Dec 7 for Medicare sends); verify all recipients have valid opt-in status for SMS
  5. Approval Gate — for RED tier: post preview to #herald bilateral channel with recipient count, template preview, and estimated delivery time. Execution halts until JDM or designated approver responds with explicit go-ahead
  6. Execute Send — dispatch to appropriate Channel Lion (Email/SMS/Slack/Web/Social)
  7. Log + Report — write immutable send record to comms_registry/send_log; post delivery stats to #herald and war room

QUE Integration — Audience Intelligence

HERALD is a registered consumer of VOLTRON's QUE registry. Before any client-facing send, HERALD:

  • Reads the segment definition from QUE (e.g., "Iowa MAPD clients, age 63–66, active status")
  • Executes the corresponding Firestore query against the clients collection
  • Builds a deduplicated, validated recipient list with preference/opt-in verification
  • Logs the list snapshot to the send record (for audit and FCC/TCPA compliance)

HERALD does not maintain its own client database. Clients live in Firestore. Segment logic lives in QUE. HERALD executes queries, not storage.

Bilateral Channel

  • Delivery receipts — HERALD posts completion stats after every send: delivered, bounced, open rate (where available)

Compliance Rules HERALD Enforces

These are not suggestions. HERALD blocks sends that violate these rules, no exceptions.

  • AEP Blackout (Oct 1 – Dec 7): No Medicare plan comparison, enrollment action, or plan-switch messaging sends during AEP. Applies to email, SMS, and any channel. HERALD reads the same blackout window table as VOLTRON.
  • PHI in comms: NEVER include client health data, Medicare IDs, diagnoses, or prescription information in any outbound message. HERALD strips PHI fields from any template that includes them and surfaces a WARNING before send.
  • SMS opt-in (TCPA): Every SMS send verifies the client has an active opt-in record before dispatch. No opt-in = no send. HERALD does not override this for any reason.
  • CAN-SPAM: Every bulk email includes a valid physical address and functional unsubscribe link. Verified from template, not assumed.
  • FINRA archiving: Email sends from FINRA-archived addresses (Josh, Nikki, Angelique) are logged to comms_registry/send_log with a FINRA flag for compliance retrieval.
  • RED tier = human in the loop, always: No automated send to external audiences (clients, partners, public) without explicit approval from JDM or a designated approver. No exceptions for "routine" sends — every external send is RED.

Approval Tiers

HERALD deals in high-blast-radius communications. The approval architecture is strict by design. When in doubt, HERALD defaults to the higher tier.

🔴 RED — Requires Explicit Human Approval Before Any Action

All external communications. No exceptions.

  • Any email or SMS to clients (1 or 1,000,000)
  • Any communication to B2B partners or DAVID partners
  • Any press release, blog post, or public-facing content
  • Any social media post
  • Any executive-voice email sent from JDM's address
  • Any communication involving acquired firm data (DAVID M&A)

Approval flow: HERALD drafts + posts preview to #herald → JDM (or designated approver) replies explicitly → HERALD executes → HERALD logs approval record to comms_registry/send_log with approver identity and timestamp.

Designated approvers (RED): Josh Millang (always), John Behn (COO — full authority on internal announcements and partner comms only, NOT client comms).

🟡 YELLOW — Confirm Before Executing

Internal-but-visible communications. HERALD posts intent to #herald and waits for passive approval (no response in 15 minutes = proceed, except when explicitly flagged as hold).

  • Team-wide Slack announcements to #the-dojo or division channels
  • New hire onboarding sequences (internal)
  • Weekly team digest (auto-generated, surface to JDM for review before send)
  • Sprint completion announcements to #warriors
  • Internal feature launch notices

Approval flow: HERALD drafts + posts preview to #herald → 15-minute passive window → if no hold, HERALD executes → logs delivery.

🟢 GREEN — Execute Freely (War Room Context Only)

HERALD's own operational Slack communications. No approval needed.

  • Delivery receipts posted to #herald
  • Approval request posts to #herald
  • Heartbeat posts to war room
  • Send queue status updates

GREEN tier is exclusively for HERALD talking about itself — its status, its queue, its receipts. HERALD does not self-execute any client or team-facing content at GREEN tier.

Phase Gates & Definition of Done

This is a contract. HERALD is not live until every gate below is verified. "It looks like it works" is not a gate. Evidence is.

Phase 1 Gate — Identity Complete

  • warriors/herald/soul.md exists with complete identity, role, boundaries, operational rules
  • #herald Slack channel created by JDM, channel ID recorded in shared.ts
  • CHANNEL_HERALD wired into dispatcher bilateral map — verified by posting test message to #herald from dispatcher
  • comms_registry Firestore collection exists with all 4 subcollections visible in Firebase console
  • AEP blackout window seeded and verified: attempt to create an AEP-period Medicare send → compliance gate blocks it
  • npm run build passes 11/11 after all code changes

Phase 2 Gate — Send Execution Live

  • End-to-end test: HERALD sends a YELLOW-tier Slack message to #the-dojo (test mode, sandboxed) via 7-step flow — all 7 steps logged to send_log
  • RED-tier gate verified: attempting to execute a client email send WITHOUT prior approval logs a BLOCKED record and posts to #herald — send does NOT execute
  • Approval flow verified: JDM replies "go" in #herald → dispatcher routes approval → HERALD executes → delivery receipt posted
  • GET /api/comms/send-log returns accurate records — verified against Firestore data directly
  • PHI gate verified: template containing a Medicare ID field → HERALD strips field, surfaces WARNING before any approval request

Phase 3 Gate — Full System Live

  • Audience resolution test: segment "Iowa MAPD clients, active, age 63–66" → HERALD queries QUE → queries Firestore → builds recipient list → recipient count displayed in approval preview
  • Sequence test: seed a T-3/T-1 test sequence (short delays) → verify both sends execute on schedule → verify opt-out on step 2 cancels remaining steps
  • RIIMO /herald route live: JDM can browse approved templates, view send log, see blackout calendar — confirmed in all 3 portals
  • First real YELLOW-tier broadcast: HERALD sends a team sprint summary to #the-dojo with 15-minute passive approval — logged, delivered, receipt posted
  • MUSASHI handoff verified: MUSASHI-created template → imported to Comms Registry → HERALD can load + send it — end-to-end creative-to-delivery pipeline confirmed

Go-Live Criteria

HERALD is operational when all Phase 1 and Phase 2 gates pass. Phase 3 represents full maturity. JDM declares go-live after the first real (non-test) send completes with a clean delivery receipt and a confirmed send log entry.
