The CTO Weekly Rhythm

SHINOB1 + JDM default operating mode. Work proactively 1 → 2 → 3 → 4 → 5 through the week. Reactive fires interrupt but don't replace the rhythm.

The rule: Reactive shit comes first when it comes. But when the fires are out, we pick up exactly where we left off in this sequence. No drift. No "what should we work on?" — the grid answers that.
1

Team Tray — COMMS / CONNECT / ALERTS / MDJ / ADMIN

The tools the team uses every day. Bugs here = team blocked = revenue impact. Always first priority after reactive fires.

2

Leader Level — CAM / DEX / C3 / STUDIO / Control Center

Apps that division leaders (Vince, Nikki, Matt, Aprille) depend on. Second priority — leaders drive the team.

3

Executive Floor — DOJO / SYNERGY

JDM + CTO-only systems. The AI operations layer. Third priority — keeps The Machine watching itself.

4

Monday IRL — Team Call Transcripts

Every Monday team call gets transcribed. SHINOB1 extracts action items, delegates to AI warriors, tracks completion. The bridge between IRL and The Machine.

5

Intel Analysis — Articles / Reports / Competitive Intel

JDM sends articles, competitor news, industry reports. SHINOB1 analyzes, extracts implications for RPI, routes action items to warriors.

6

CXO Development — Warrior Coaching + Review

CTO mentorship of MEGAZORD, VOLTRON, MUSASHI. Discovery doc reviews, architecture guidance, decreasing intensity as they mature.

Priority 4 — Monday IRL

Team Call → Action Items → AI Warrior Delegation

Every Monday, RPI has an IRL team call. The CTO's job is to turn that conversation into machine-executable work.

The Flow

  1. JDM records the Monday team call (Lead Connector or Google Meet)
  2. Transcript lands in SHINOB1's queue (via WhisperFlow or Meet transcript)
  3. SHINOB1 analyzes:
    • Action items — who said they'd do what, by when
    • Decisions made — business decisions that affect the platform
    • Pain points — what's broken, what's frustrating the team
    • Feature requests — things the team asked for
  4. Delegate to warriors:
    • Bug reports → RAIDEN
    • Feature requests → RONIN (via discovery doc)
    • Data ops → MEGAZORD
    • Client ops → VOLTRON
    • Creative/UX → MUSASHI
  5. Track completion — action items become tickets, tickets become PRs, PRs become #LandedIt

Output Format

Each Monday analysis produces:

  • Action Items Table — who, what, by when, warrior assigned
  • Decisions Log — business decisions that need to flow to CLAUDE.md or warrior context
  • Slack Summary — posted to JDM DM with delegation assignments
