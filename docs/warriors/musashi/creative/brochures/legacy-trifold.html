Legacy · Step by Step

Three Legacy Conversations

Every legacy plan runs through the same three conversations. Have all three before a crisis forces them.

Conversation 1

The document conversation

Will, Living Trust, Durable Power of Attorney, Healthcare Directive. Everyone needs all four. Having one or two is not enough.

Conversation 2

The beneficiary conversation

Named beneficiaries on IRAs, 401(k)s, life insurance, and annuities override your will. Audit every account every year — life changes fast.

Conversation 3

The tax conversation

Federal + state estate tax, income tax on inherited retirement accounts, and step-up in basis for taxable accounts. The rules matter and they change.

Inside · Panel 2
The Four Essential Documents

What Every Adult Needs

Document 1

Last Will & Testament

Directs non-designated assets. Names guardians for minors. Goes through probate.

Document 2

Living Trust

Avoids probate. Privacy + speed. Revocable or Irrevocable — choose with your attorney.

Document 3

Durable POA

Names someone to manage finances if you’re incapacitated. Durable = survives incapacity.

Document 4

Healthcare Directive

Documents end-of-life wishes. Names a healthcare agent. Critical for ICU scenarios.

Trust Types at a Glance

Revocable Living — Avoids probate. No estate tax/creditor protection.
Irrevocable — Out of estate. Tax + asset protection. Hard to change.
Special Needs — Preserves government benefit eligibility.
CRT — Income to grantor; remainder to charity. Tax benefits.
ILIT — Removes life insurance from taxable estate.
Testamentary — Created by will at death. Useful for minor children.

The Annual Review

Birthday or tax time: review beneficiaries, update documents, ask about SECURE Act changes. One hour.

Life Events That Require Updates

  • Marriage or divorce
  • Birth or adoption
  • Death of a beneficiary
  • Significant asset change
  • Move to a new state
  • Business sale or formation
  • Adult child’s divorce
  • Health diagnosis
  • Inheritance
