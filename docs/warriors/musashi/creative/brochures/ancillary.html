Ancillary · Filling the Real Gaps

What Medicare Won't Cover.

Dental, vision, hearing, hospital indemnity, final expense, long-term care — the coverage that completes the plan.

Original Medicare covers medically necessary hospital and doctor care. It doesn't cover routine dental, vision, hearing, or long-term custodial care. And even with Medicare Advantage or Medigap, real-world costs — a hospital stay, a nursing home, dentures — can hit hard. This guide walks you through the six categories that fill those gaps.

Gap 1

The daily gap

Dental, vision, hearing — Medicare doesn't cover these routinely. Standalone and bundled plans exist that go to work right away.

Gap 2

The catastrophic gap

Long-term care costs $90K+/year. Medicare covers only 100 days of skilled care — and only up to a point. The rest is your bill.

Gap 3

The final gap

Burial, cremation, medical debt at death. Final Expense Life covers what Medicare doesn't — and keeps the burden off your family.

The Six Ancillary Categories

Dental / Vision / Hearing

Routine & Preventive Care

Cleanings, exams, hearing aids — Medicare skips all of it. Preventive benefits often covered day-1. Major dental (crowns, dentures) typically has a waiting period. Standalone or bundled with MA plans.

Hospital Indemnity

Fixed Daily Benefit

Pays a fixed daily amount directly to the insured for inpatient hospital stays. Stackable on top of Medicare Advantage or Medigap — no coordination of benefits required. Benefits used any way you choose.

Final Expense

Small Whole Life Policy

Face amounts of $5K–$25K. Covers burial, cremation, medical debt at death. Simplified underwriting — shorter health questionnaire, no exam required. Premiums never increase. Benefit never decreases.

Long-Term Care

Custodial Care Coverage

Assisted living, nursing home, and in-home aide. Medicare covers only skilled nursing (max 100 days) — not custodial. LTC insurance kicks in when you can no longer perform 2 of 6 Activities of Daily Living.

Home Health Care

In-Home Provider Benefits

Benefits paid directly to an in-home care provider for physical, speech, or respiratory therapy. Fills the gap when Medicare's home health benefit runs out or doesn't qualify. Care on your schedule, in your home.

Recovery Care

Post-Hospital Skilled Nursing

Skilled, intermediate, or custodial nursing coverage after a qualifying hospital stay. Bridges the gap between hospital discharge and return to full function. Works alongside Medicare Part A skilled nursing benefit.

Underwriting Reality Check

Not every ancillary product has the same underwriting hurdle. The harder the underwriting, the more important it is to apply early — while your health history is clean.

Product Underwriting Type Health Questions Best Time to Apply
Dental / Vision / HearingGuaranteed Issue (at enrollment)NoneAt 65 / plan change
Hospital IndemnitySimplified Issue1–3 questionsAnytime
Final ExpenseSimplified or Guaranteed Issue0–5 questionsAnytime; sooner = lower premium
Long-Term CareFull Medical UnderwritingFull health history50s–60s (before diagnosis)
Home Health / Recovery CareSimplified IssueADL trigger questionsPre-retirement; early 60s

ADL = Activities of Daily Living (bathing, dressing, eating, transferring, continence, toileting). Most LTC and home health policies trigger benefits when you cannot perform 2 of 6 ADLs. LTC denial rate increases significantly after age 70 or after a major health event.

When You Can Enroll

Most ancillary products have open enrollment year-round — not tied to AEP. Exceptions: Medicare Advantage plans with bundled dental/vision/hearing benefits follow AEP rules (Oct 15 — Dec 7). Standalone ancillary policies can be added anytime you qualify medically.

Conditions That Trigger Each Product

  • Turning 65 (initial Medicare enrollment)
  • Retirement or loss of employer coverage
  • New mortgage or major family event
  • Nursing home admission
  • Hospital stay over 3 days
  • New chronic illness diagnosis
  • Spouse passes away
  • Loss of a spouse's group coverage
  • Move to assisted living or memory care
  • Decline of 2+ Activities of Daily Living
  • Financial planning / estate review
  • Qualifying life event (contact us)
