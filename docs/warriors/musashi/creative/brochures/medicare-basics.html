Medicare isn't supposed to be a test. It's a system that protects you in retirement — but only if you understand what's yours, what's missing, and what it'll cost. This guide walks you through the three decisions every Medicare-eligible client faces, plus the numbers that matter this year.

Step 1

Pick your coverage path

Original Medicare (Parts A + B) OR a Medicare Advantage Plan (Part C, which bundles A+B and usually D).

Step 2

Fill the gaps

On Original Medicare, consider a Medigap policy to cover what Part A and B don't. (On Advantage, Medigap is not allowed.)

Step 3

Secure drug coverage

Add a standalone Part D plan, OR confirm your Medicare Advantage plan includes it. Missing this window costs you.

The Four Parts of Medicare

Part A

Hospital Insurance

Inpatient hospital stays, skilled nursing, hospice, and some home health care. Most people pay no premium if they or a spouse paid Medicare taxes long enough.

Part B

Medical Insurance

Doctor visits, outpatient care, preventive services, and medical equipment. Monthly premium applies; can be higher based on income.

Part C

Medicare Advantage

A bundled plan (like an HMO or PPO) offered by a private carrier. Includes Part A + B, usually Part D, and often extras (dental, vision, fitness).

Part D

Prescription Drug Coverage

Standalone drug plan or bundled with Advantage. Missing your initial enrollment window means a lifetime late-enrollment penalty.

Medigap — Standardized Plans

Annual Enrollment Period (AEP)

Oct 15 — Dec 7 every year. The window to join, switch, or drop a Medicare Advantage or Part D plan. Changes take effect January 1. Miss it and you're locked in.

Special Election Periods (SEP) — when you can change outside AEP

  • Turning 65 (Initial Enrollment Period)
  • Qualifying for Extra Help on prescriptions
  • Leaving employer group / creditable coverage
  • Moving outside your plan's coverage area
  • Dual-eligible (Medicare & Medicaid)
  • Your plan earns a 5-star rating
  • Involuntary loss of coverage
  • Qualifying life event (contact us)
