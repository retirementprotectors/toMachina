Life Insurance, Demystified.

Term, Permanent, Whole, Universal — the real differences and when each makes sense.

Life insurance isn't one product — it's a toolkit. Term is rent. Permanent is ownership. Whole is a guaranteed floor. Universal is flexibility. This guide walks you through each type so you can match the right tool to the protection you actually need.

Step 1

Know what you're protecting

Income replacement · Mortgage · College · Estate · Final expense · Business continuation. Define the need before picking the product.

Step 2

Pick the right type

Term for temporary needs. Permanent for lifetime needs. Hybrid strategies can blend both when the protection horizon is mixed.

Step 3

Review annually

Premiums, cash value, dividends, and beneficiaries change — and so does the right policy. An annual check keeps coverage aligned with life.

Life Insurance Types

Term Life

Coverage for a Set Period

10, 15, 20, or 30-year terms. Lowest premium of any type. No cash value accumulates. Return-of-Premium (ROP) variants refund premiums paid if you outlive the term.

Whole Life

Permanent + Guaranteed

Coverage for life with a guaranteed premium, guaranteed cash value growth, and guaranteed death benefit. Participating policies earn dividends that can reduce premiums or grow cash value.

Universal Life

Permanent + Flexible

Permanent coverage with adjustable premiums and a flexible death benefit. Cash value earns interest based on type: Fixed (declared rate), Indexed (index-linked), or Variable (investment sub-accounts).

Final Expense

Simplified Whole Life

Small whole-life policies ($5K–$50K) designed for burial and final medical costs. Simplified underwriting — no medical exam. Guaranteed issue options available for harder-to-insure applicants.

Policy Performance Review

A policy that made sense five years ago may not make sense today. Markets shift, mortality tables update, dividend scales change, and life happens. These are the six triggers that tell us a policy needs a second look.

Review Trigger What to Look For
Death Benefit Options Level, increasing, or return-of-premium — does the structure still match the original goal?
Cost of Insurance Changes 2017 CSO mortality tables updated — people live longer now, and carrier pricing has shifted accordingly.
Interest Rate Trends Sustained low-rate environments cause UL and VUL policies to underperform original projections.
Lowered Dividend Scales Participating whole life dividend scales have declined at most carriers. Run a current in-force illustration.
Life Changes Beneficiary designations, coverage amounts, and ownership structures often go years without a review.
Gap in Service Agent retired, company merged, or no annual reviews in years — who is watching your policy?
New Coverage Choices Guaranteed lifetime coverage with LTC riders and hybrid products now exist that didn't 10 years ago.

Life Changes That Trigger a Review

Marriage · Divorce · Birth or adoption · New mortgage · Retirement · Business ownership · Health diagnosis · Beneficiary death. Any of these means your coverage should be re-evaluated now.

When to Consider Each Type

  • Young family + mortgage → Term (high coverage, low cost)
  • Lifetime protection needed → Whole Life (guaranteed + dividends)
  • Flexible premium payer → Universal Life
  • Tax-efficient wealth transfer → Permanent + ILIT
  • Final expense only → Final Expense Whole Life
  • Business owner → Key-Person or Buy-Sell Agreement
