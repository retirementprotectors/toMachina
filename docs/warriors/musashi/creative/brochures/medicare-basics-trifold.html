Medicare · Step by Step

Three Decisions

Every Medicare-eligible person walks through the same three choices. Here's how each one works.

Step 1

Pick your coverage path

Original Medicare (Parts A + B, run by the federal government) OR a Medicare Advantage Plan (Part C, bundled by a private carrier, usually includes Part D).

Step 2

Fill the gaps

On Original Medicare, consider a Medigap policy to cover what A and B don't. On Medicare Advantage, Medigap is not allowed.

Step 3

Secure drug coverage

Add a standalone Part D plan, OR confirm your Advantage plan includes it. Missing the window means a lifetime late-enrollment penalty.

Inside · Panel 2
The Four Parts

What Each Part Covers

Part A

Hospital

Inpatient stays, skilled nursing, hospice, some home health. Usually $0 premium.

Part B

Medical

Doctor visits, outpatient, preventive, equipment. Monthly premium applies.

Part C

Advantage

Private plan bundling A + B, usually D, often extras (dental, vision, fitness).

Part D

Drugs

Prescription coverage. Miss enrollment = lifetime penalty.
