Life Insurance · Step by Step

Three Steps to the Right Policy

Every life insurance decision comes down to three questions. Here’s how to answer them.

Step 1

Know what you're protecting

Income replacement · Mortgage balance · College funding · Estate taxes · Final expenses · Business continuation. Define the need first.

Step 2

Pick the right type

Term for temporary needs (high coverage, low cost). Permanent for lifetime needs. Hybrid strategies blend both when the protection horizon is mixed.

Step 3

Review annually

Premiums, cash value, dividends, and beneficiary designations change over time — and so does the right policy. Annual reviews keep coverage aligned with life.

The Four Types

What Each Type Does

Term Life

Set Period

10–30 yr terms. Lowest premium. No cash value. ROP variants available.

Whole Life

Permanent + Guaranteed

Guaranteed premium, cash value, and death benefit. Participating policies earn dividends.

Universal Life

Permanent + Flexible

Adjustable premiums and death benefit. Fixed, Indexed, or Variable cash value growth.

Final Expense

Simplified Whole Life

$5K–$50K coverage. No medical exam. Guaranteed issue options available.

Inside · Panel 3
Review + Match

Policy Performance Triggers

Review Your Policy If…

  • Death benefit structure no longer matches your goal
  • Cost of insurance has increased (2017 CSO update)
  • UL/VUL may be underperforming original projections
  • Dividend scale has been lowered on your whole life
  • Beneficiary designations haven’t been touched in years
  • Your agent retired or company was acquired
  • LTC riders or hybrid products are now available to you

Life Changes = Review Triggers

Marriage · Divorce · Birth · Mortgage · Retirement · Business ownership · Health diagnosis · Beneficiary death

When to Consider Each Type

  • Young family + mortgage → Term
  • Lifetime protection → Whole Life
  • Flexible premium payer → Universal Life
  • Tax-efficient wealth transfer → Permanent + ILIT
  • Final expense only → Final Expense Whole Life
  • Business owner → Key-Person / Buy-Sell
