RPI Brand Style Guide

The new brand standard. Warm + professional. Universal across print, digital, portal, email, F2F. One voice, five facets. This doc governs every RPI-branded piece shipped after 2026-04-12.

1. The One-Line

We help families navigate Medicare, retirement, life, legacy — with the warmth of a friend and the precision of a pro.

Every brochure, page, email, and handout expresses this. If it doesn't — rewrite.

2. Voice

AttributeRule
WarmWrite like the person across the kitchen table. Use contractions. Say "we" and "you," not "the client."
ProfessionalNo jargon traps. Define terms the first time. Avoid hype words (amazing, incredible, life-changing).
DirectLead with the point. Bullet the benefits. Cut every word that doesn't earn its spot.
Dual-purposeReads naturally to a client AND works as an agent selling tool. No content that only makes sense one-way.
HonestSurface the catch. "Original Medicare doesn't cover everything — here's what fills the gap" beats "Medicare is awesome."

Voice in context

Headline — Medicare

❌ "Optimize Your Healthcare Strategy with Expert Medicare Guidance"

✅ "Medicare, Made Simple."

Subhead — Life Insurance

❌ "Comprehensive Insurance Solutions for Your Family's Future"

✅ "The people you love shouldn't have to figure this out alone."

CTA

❌ "Contact us today for a consultation."

✅ "Schedule a no-cost review. Bring your questions."

3. Tagline & Trademarks

We're Your People™ — the trademark. Use with the ™ mark. This is the master tagline for every piece.

MYST — Meet Your Service Team — the team page concept.

#RunningOurOwnRACE — internal/culture tagline. Sparingly external.

7. Imagery

TypeWhenRule
Real photographyTeam, client meetings, officesProfessional quality only. Warm lighting. Authentic expressions — not stock-model smiles.
Stock photographyConcepts (retirement, family, security)Unsplash / Canva stock. Avoid cliché (gold coins, stethoscope on money). Diverse representation.
IllustrationProcesses, diagrams, stepsClean, minimal, flat. No heavy gradients. Consistent line weight.
IconsBenefits, features, stepsSingle family per piece (e.g., Heroicons or Lucide). Same weight and style throughout.

8. Layout Rules

  • Grid: 12-col or 3-col tri-fold. No freestyle layouts.
  • Whitespace: Generous. Crowded brochures look untrustworthy. 40% white is a floor, not a ceiling.
  • Hierarchy: Hero headline → subhead → 3 benefit bullets → proof/data → CTA. Every piece.
  • Contact block: Footer on every piece: RetireProtected.com | 515-992-5000 | 877-650-7015 | Service@RetireProtected.com
  • Trademark: We're Your People™ appears on every client-facing piece.

12. Enforcement

Say it TO, not ABOUT: Every tool, interface, and message is addressed directly to the person in front of it. Never talk down to the team. Never talk past the client.
Mouth to machine: From JDM's mouth to the platform. No translation loss. No watered-down compromise.
NEVER generate visual assets (logos, icons, marks, custom SVGs). Use authorized assets only. JDM art-directs.
