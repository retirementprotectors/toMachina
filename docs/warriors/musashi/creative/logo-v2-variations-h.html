H-ish: The Big-Shield Horizontal Lockup

You liked H but the small shield baked into the wordmark was a problem. Here are six refinements — the big standalone shield REPLACES the small one entirely, paired with clean "RETIREMENT PROTECTORS, INC." text and the tagline underneath.

What changed from the original H

Old H: Big shield on left AND the small shield baked into the existing wordmark = two shields, confusing.

All six H-ish variants: wordmark is cropped to TEXT ONLY (x=532 onward), small shield removed cleanly. Big shield on the left stands alone. Zero duplicated marks.

Vary: shield scale (1.0x – 2.0x), gap between shield and text, tagline size/weight, tagline alignment (left / center / right under the text).

H1 — Balanced

Shield 1.5x · Bold 120 centered · 80px gap
The balanced starting point. Shield has presence, text reads cleanly, tagline centers under the wordmark.

H2 — Shield Prominent

Shield 1.8x · tight 60px gap
Shield becomes the hero. Closer spacing keeps the lockup feeling like one unit.

H3 — Refined Match

Shield 1.3x · SemiBold 110 · 70px gap
Shield matches the visual weight of the wordmark. SemiBold tagline sits respectful, not shouting.

H4 — Dominant Shield + Left Tagline

Shield 2.0x · tagline left under "Retirement"
Biggest shield of the bunch. Tagline left-aligned under "RETIREMENT" feels like a byline.

H5 — Tight Integration

Shield matches wordmark HEIGHT · 40px gap · Bold 96
Shield height = text height. Most integrated, feels like a classic corporate lockup.

H6 — Right-Aligned Tagline

Shield 1.4x · tagline right under "INC."
Tagline right-aligns to close out the mark. Executive / legal-serious feel.
