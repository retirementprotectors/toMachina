🔴 FRA TIMING — SEPT/OCT 2026 FILING IS PRE-FRA = PERMANENT BENEFIT REDUCTION

If Chris is born 1960 (baseline): Full Retirement Age per SSA is 67 exact — reached late 2027. Filing Sept/Oct 2026 at age 66 is ~12-14 months BEFORE FRA, which triggers a permanent ~6.67-7.5% benefit reduction under the SSA early-claim penalty.

Impact on the projected $2,400/mo:

  • Filing Sept 2026 (13 months pre-FRA): ~6.7% reduction → ~$2,240/mo lifetime ($160/mo = $1,920/yr forever)
  • Waiting to FRA (Oct 2027): $2,400/mo clean
  • Delaying past FRA to age 70 (Oct 2030): +8%/yr delayed retirement credit = ~$2,976/mo ($576/mo more than FRA filing, $736/mo more than the early-file path)

Breakeven math (simple): 13 months of forgone benefits from waiting FRA vs filing now = ~$31K. Recovered in ~16 years of the $160/mo differential — breakeven around age 83. For a healthy 66-year-old with family longevity, waiting to FRA is mathematically correct. Delaying past FRA to age 70 breaks even in ~12-13 years.

The real question: Does Chris NEED the income in 2026? His current ~$83K household income suggests no. If no cash-flow need, file at FRA (~Oct 2027) at minimum; consider age 70 delay if health + longevity support it.
