The Key Insight

The GMIB withdrawal is taxable regardless of how the funds are used. Whether you take the income, reinvest it, or fund life insurance — the tax bill is the same. The only question is:

What delivers the greatest after-tax value to your family?

Every dollar directed to the John Hancock policy creates tax-free death benefit leverage. The IRS taxes the withdrawal, but the proceeds your family receives are 100% income tax-free under IRC §101(a). This is the most efficient use of taxable IRA dollars available.

What This Means

To beat Option A, you would need to earn 7.7% annually, after fees, every single year for 24 consecutive years — and your children would still pay income tax on whatever is left in the IRA.

With Option A, you pay ~$35,000 per year from existing bank reserves and hand the next generation $6,579,631 completely tax-free.

Why the IRA Makes This Even More Compelling
If You Keep the Money in the IRA
  • Growth is tax-deferred, not tax-free
  • Heirs pay income tax on every dollar inherited
  • Must be distributed within 10 years (SECURE Act)
