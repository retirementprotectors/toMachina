1DeDup — Managing Duplicates

The system automatically detects potential duplicate contacts. Here's how to handle them.

Spotting Duplicates

warning

Orange "Dupe" Badge on Contact Grid

When browsing the Contacts list, some contacts will have an orange Dupe badge next to their name. This means the system found another contact with the same email, phone, or last name + date of birth.

expand_more

"Possible Duplicates" Banner on Contact Detail

When you open a contact that has potential duplicates, you'll see a collapsible orange banner below the header. Click it to expand and see the matches with their confidence scores.

Understanding Match Scores

priority_high

95% — Same Email

Two contacts share the exact same email address. Almost certainly a duplicate.

report

90% — Same Phone

Two contacts share the same phone number. Very likely a duplicate or household member.

info

85% — Same Last Name + DOB

Matching last name and date of birth. Could be a duplicate or a family member. Review carefully.

Taking Action

block

Dismiss — It's Not a Duplicate

Click Dismiss on any match that isn't actually a duplicate. The match will disappear and won't show up again. Dismissal is saved permanently.

merge_type

Merge — Confirmed Duplicate

Select the duplicate(s) with the checkboxes and click "DeDup Selected". This opens the merge tool where you can compare field-by-field and choose which data to keep.

2Relationships — Connecting People

Build connections between contacts and organize them into households.

Adding Connections

person_add

Step 1: Go to the Relationships Tab

Open a contact's detail page and click the Relationships tab. You'll see existing connections and options to add new ones.

search

Step 2: Search for a Person

Click "Add Connection", select a relationship type (Spouse, Child, Parent, etc.), and search by name. The system searches all contacts in the database.

link

Step 3: Choose Your Action

Each search result shows three buttons:

link

Connect

Creates a bidirectional connection between the two contacts. Both records are updated. Use this when the people know each other but don't live together.

home

+ HH (Add to Household)

Adds the person to the current contact's household. Use this for people who live together or share finances. Note: The current contact must already be in a household.

done_all

Both

Creates the connection AND adds to household in one click. Use this for family members who live together — it's the most common action for spouses and children.

Recommended Matches

auto_awesome

System Suggestions

The system automatically suggests connections based on shared email addresses, phone numbers, or similar names. Review these suggestions at the bottom of the Relationships tab. The same three action buttons (Connect, + HH, Both) are available on each suggestion.

3Household AI3 Reports

Generate comprehensive household-level intelligence reports.

psychology

What is AI3?

AI3 (Automated Integrated Intelligence & Insights) generates a comprehensive report that pulls together data from ALL members of a household — accounts, financials, opportunities, and action items.

touch_app

How to Generate

Navigate to a Household detail page and click the "AI3 Report" button in the header. The report generates in a few seconds and opens in a modal.

summarize

Understanding the Report

The report includes:

  • Member Inventory — All household members with their roles and account counts
  • Aggregate Financials — Combined income, net worth, and investable assets
  • Cross-Member Opportunities — Insurance gaps, consolidation possibilities, planning needs
  • Action Items — Specific tasks for each member, prioritized by impact
